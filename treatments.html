Treatment Options:




Voxelotor (medication)


This medication is used in adults and children older than 4 years old. This medication helps to prevent red blood cells from turning into a sickle shape. Helps reduce risk of anemia and improves blood flow to organs.

Price: $12,044 for 60 30 mg tablets.

Covered by Insurance: YES

Side Effects: headaches, diarrhea, abdominal pain, nausea, fatigue and fever.


Crizanlizumab-tmca (medication)


This is for adults and children 16 years or older. This is given to the patient through an IV (intravenous line) This helps prevent blood cells from sticking to the blood vessel walls. This inturn prevents blood flow blockage and inflammation.

Price: about $2,592 per 10 milliliters

Covered by Insurance: Depends on circumstances

Side Effects: nausea, joint pain, back pain and fever.


L-glutamine (amino acid)


This is a medication meant for those older than the age of 5 and is used to prevent pain crises. This is prescribed as a powder that should be mixed into a liquid or food.

Price: $28.73 per 1,226 GM of powder

Covered by Insurance: Only when medically necessary

Side Effects: nausea, fatigue, chest pain and musculoskeletal pain.


Ibuprofen (medication)


Used to treat mild and moderate pain, inflammation, and joint pain.

Price: $11.35 per 120 pills

Covered by Insurance: No

Side Effects: headache, dizziness, drowsiness, ringing ears, numbness in hands and feet, and blurred vision.


Acetaminophen (medication)


Used to treat mild and moderate pain, it can also be used to reduce fever.

Price: $6.26 per 125 caplets

Covered by Insurance: No

Side Effects: rash, hives, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing.


Penicillin (antibiotic)


This is an antibiotic that can be used to help reduce risk of infection in the bloodstream for children.

Price: $20.80 - $27.29 per 40 tablets

Covered by Insurance: Yes

Side Effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, rash, abdominal pain, and urticaria.


Transfusions:

  • Acute transfusions are used to treat complications that are caused by severe anemia.
  • Red blood cell transfusions increase the amount of red blood cells in the patient and also provides them with normal red blood cells.
  • Regular or ongoing blood transfusions are used to reduce the risk of a stroke in those that have already had an acute stroke.
